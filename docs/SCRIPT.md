# Introduction: Storytelling and the Goals of Humanity

Humanity is always telling a story about what kind of world it believes is possible. We speak of ending suffering, widening dignity, protecting the earth, sharing knowledge, building peace, and leaving those who come after us a life not diminished by our choices. These are not merely ambitions; they are the shape of a common human hope.

But hope does not move through the world by argument alone. It needs story. Storytelling turns distant problems into felt realities, private lives into shared meaning, and abstract ideals into choices a person can recognize as their own. Through story, we come to understand not only what is broken, but what might yet be repaired.

To tell better stories is to expand the horizon of action. A story can awaken responsibility, give courage to cooperation, and help people see themselves not as spectators of history, but as authors within it. In this way, storytelling does not merely describe the goals of humanity; it helps bring them into being.
